MORE POPULAR IELTS SPEAKING TOPICS 2025 [+KEY VOCABULARY]

Topic 1: Travel and Transport

Transport Vocabulary

  • Forms / means / modes of transport - ways of travelling from one place to another.
    • Example: There are many means of transport such as buses and trains.
  • To travel by car / by plane / by train / on foot - describe how you move from place to place.
    • Example: I prefer to travel by train because it‚Äôs more comfortable.
  • Public transport (UK) / public transportation (US) - transport used by everyone, like buses or the metro.
    • Example: I prefer public transport as it helps reduce pollution.
  • Private transport (UK) / private transportation (US) - your own vehicle such as a car or motorbike.
    • Example: Private transport is convenient but quite expensive.
  • Global warming - the rise in Earth‚Äôs temperature caused by pollution.
    • Example: Cars contribute a lot to global warming.
  • Carbon footprint - the amount of carbon dioxide you produce.
    • Example: Flying increases your carbon footprint.
  • To offset your carbon footprint - to reduce or balance the pollution you cause.
    • Example: You can offset your carbon footprint by planting trees.
  • Sustainable vehicles - eco-friendly forms of transport.
    • Example: Electric bikes are a good example of sustainable vehicles.
  • Zero-emission vehicles - cars that produce no pollution.
    • Example: Zero-emission vehicles are becoming more and more common.
  • Driverless cars - cars controlled by computers rather than humans.
    • Example: Driverless cars will certainly become more popular in the future.

Travel to Work

  • Daily commute - your regular journey to work or school.
    • Example: My daily commute takes about thirty minutes.
  • Rush hour / peak hours - the busiest travel times of the day.
    • Example: Traffic is awful during rush hour.
  • Traffic jam - a long line of cars not moving.
    • Example: I was stuck in a traffic jam for over an hour.
  • To be stuck in traffic - unable to move because of heavy traffic.
    • Example: I‚Äôm sorry I‚Äôm late, I was stuck in traffic on the way here.
  • Bumper to bumper - cars very close together in traffic.
    • Example: Traffic in the rush hour is often bumper to bumper.
  • Air pollution - dirty air caused by vehicles or factories.
    • Example: Air pollution is a serious problem in big cities.
  • Exhaust fumes - gas from car engines.
    • Example: Exhaust fumes damage the environment.

Holidays and Tourism

  • To go on a trip - to travel somewhere for a short time.
    • Example: We went on a trip to Italy last year.
  • To book a holiday / hotel - to reserve your travel or accommodation.
    • Example: I booked a hotel online yesterday.
  • A package holiday - a holiday organised by a travel company.
    • Example: I prefer package holidays because everything is taken care of.
  • To see the sights / go sightseeing - to visit famous places.
    • Example: We spent the weekend sightseeing in Paris.
  • To stay in a hotel - to sleep in a hotel.
    • Example: We stayed in a lovely hotel near the beach.
  • Holiday destination - a popular place to visit.
    • Example: Bali is a famous holiday destination.
  • To take a flight / long-haul flight - to fly to another place far away.
    • Example: I took a long-haul flight to Australia.
  • To suffer from jet lag - to feel tired after a long flight.
    • Example: I usually suffer from jet lag when I fly to Asia.
  • Mass tourism - large numbers of tourists visiting one place.
    • Example: Mass tourism can harm local culture.
  • Eco-tourism - responsible travel that protects nature.
    • Example: Eco-tourism supports local communities.
  • Domestic travel - travel within your own country.
    • Example: Domestic travel became more popular during the lockdowns.
  • Budget travel / to travel on a budget - to travel cheaply.
    • Example: Students often travel on a budget.
  • To go backpacking - to travel with a backpack, often on a budget.
    • Example: Many young people go backpacking after university.
  • Affordable travel options - cheap and accessible ways to travel.
    • Example: Low-cost airlines offer affordable travel options.

    • Example: Reading is my favourite form of relaxation.
  • A creative outlet ‚Äî a way to express creativity.
    • Example: Writing gives me a creative outlet.
  • To find pleasure in (doing something) ‚Äî to enjoy it.
    • Example: I find pleasure in gardening.
  • To get back to nature ‚Äî to spend time outdoors close to nature.
